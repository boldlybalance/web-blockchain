DeFi 101: Your Guide to Decentralized Finance

Banks take your deposits and lend them out at higher rates. DeFi lets you be the bank—and keep the spread. Here's how to start.

March 29, 2026

The Old System vs. The New

Traditional finance is built on intermediaries: banks, brokers, custodians. Every transaction passes through layers of permission, verification, and fees. DeFi removes those middlemen using smart contracts on public blockchains.

The total value locked (TVL) in DeFi protocols exceeds $180 billion as of March 2026. Ethereum remains the dominant chain with 60%+ market share, but Solana, Arbitrum, and Base have carved out significant niches.

Core DeFi Primitives

Lending & Borrowing

Protocols like Aave and Compound allow you to earn interest by supplying assets to liquidity pools—or borrow against your crypto without selling it. Interest rates are algorithmic, adjusting based on demand.

Key stat: Aave has facilitated over $35 billion in cumulative loans and currently earns depositors 3-8% APY on stablecoins, far exceeding traditional savings accounts.

Decentralized Exchanges (DEXs)

Instead of trading through a centralized exchange (Coinbase, Binance), DEXs like Uniswap and Curve use automated market makers (AMMs). You trade directly from your wallet—assets never leave your control.

Liquidity providers (LPs) deposit paired assets into pools and earn a share of trading fees. Impermanent loss is the main risk: when asset prices diverge, LP positions can lose value relative to holding.

Stablecoins

The backbone of DeFi. Stablecoins like USDC and USDT are pegged 1:1 to the US dollar, providing price stability in a volatile ecosystem. Most DeFi activity happens in stablecoins—earn yield, not exposure.

Yield Farming

The practice of maximizing returns by moving capital between protocols. Start with stablecoins → supply to Aave for 5% → bridge to Curve for 7% → stake CRV tokens for 12% boosted returns. Complexity scales with reward potential.

Getting Started Safely

1. Get a Wallet

MetaMask is the standard. Browser extension + mobile app. Write down your seed phrase—paper, metal, whatever. Never share it. Anyone with those 12-24 words owns your funds.

2. Start Small

Don't deploy life savings into a fresh protocol. Start with $50-100 on a well-audited project. Learn the interface, transaction confirmations, and fee structures before going bigger.

3. Understand the Risks

  • Smart contract risk — Code exploits, though audits reduce this
  • Impermanent loss — LP positions can lose value vs. holding
  • Regulatory risk — DeFi protocols may face restrictions in some jurisdictions
  • Rug pulls — Use established protocols; check for admin keys, timelocks

The Bottom Line

DeFi isn't magic—it's infrastructure. It offers real yield from real economic activity, not Ponzi-style "APY" that requires constant new entrants. Stick to battle-tested protocols, understand what you're doing, and never invest more than you can afford to lose.

The financial system is being rebuilt in public. Whether you're a passive earner or an active strategist, there's a seat at the table.
